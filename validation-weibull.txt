================================================================================
1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

The Weibull Analysis Tool v11.1 is a single-file HTML/JavaScript web application
providing 2-parameter and 3-parameter Weibull reliability analysis. It implements
MLE via L-BFGS-B, linear regression, Monte Carlo GoF, parametric bootstrap,
profile likelihood and Fisher confidence intervals, and the Self & Liang LR test.

The original v11.0 audit identified two CRITICAL defects, two MODERATE defects,
and four MINOR defects. Version 11.1 addresses all CRITICAL defects and two of
the MINOR defects. The remaining open items are MODERATE or MINOR in severity.

CHANGES APPLIED IN v11.1 (remediation summary):

  [DEFECT-01 - RESOLVED] ADstatistic() formula corrected. The cross-indexing
  error (Fi[i] paired with Fi[n-1-i]) has been replaced with the canonical
  formula using the same index i for both log-F and log(1-F) terms, matching
  the formula already used in weibullMLE(). Monte Carlo GoF results are now
  mathematically valid.

  [DEFECT-02 - RESOLVED] The misleading Marsaglia (2004) analytical AD p-value
  has been replaced with Stephens (1974) Weibull-specific critical value bands.
  The displayed statistic is now labeled "AD A²*" (the Stephens-corrected value),
  and the significance column shows interpretive bands (e.g., "p > 0.25 (good
  fit)", "0.05 < p < 0.10", "p < 0.01 (poor fit)") derived from the correct
  Weibull distribution reference table. The traffic light logic is updated
  accordingly.

  [DEFECT-09 - RESOLVED] Pre-filled failure times in the form on page load.
  The textarea now opens empty, as expected for a blank analysis session.

  [NEW FEATURE] "New" button added to the left of "Load Excel". Clicking it
  (with confirmation) resets all form fields to defaults, clears all results
  panels, resets chart data, and empties the analysis history — returning the
  tool to its initial state without requiring a page reload.

The tool, as shipped in v11.1, has no remaining CRITICAL defects. The two open
MODERATE defects (DEFECT-03: 3P golden-section bracket width; DEFECT-04: eta
bias correction) and three open MINOR defects (DEFECT-05: EU parsing edge case;
DEFECT-06: Excel import behavior; DEFECT-08: large-value overflow guard) do not
affect the correctness of results for typical engineering datasets.

OVERALL RATING: 9.0 / 10  (revised from 7.5/10 for v11.0)
  Remaining deductions: -0.5 for DEFECT-03 (3P golden-section bracket)
                        -0.3 for DEFECT-04 (eta bias correction absent)
                        -0.2 for minor open items
  Strengths: All critical statistical outputs are now correct. Excellent
             optimizer, valid GoF framework, correct CIs, well-structured
             code, good usability.

4.1  WEIBULL DISTRIBUTION CORE FUNCTIONS
-----------------------------------------
Functions: weibullPDF, weibullCDF, weibullHazard, weibullReliability,
           weibullQuantile

SOURCE:
  f(t) = (beta/eta) * ((t-gamma)/eta)^(beta-1) * exp(-((t-gamma)/eta)^beta)
  F(t) = 1 - exp(-((t-gamma)/eta)^beta)
  h(t) = (beta/eta) * ((t-gamma)/eta)^(beta-1)
  R(t) = exp(-((t-gamma)/eta)^beta)
  Qp   = gamma + eta * (-ln(1-p))^(1/beta)

ASSESSMENT: CORRECT. All formulas match the standard 3-parameter Weibull
definitions (Meeker & Escobar 1998, Chapter 4). Guard conditions (ts <= 0
returns 0/1) are correctly applied. Boundary handling is consistent.

STATUS: No change in v11.1. Verified correct in v11.0 audit.

VALIDATION (Dataset 1, beta=5.578471, eta=1601.3967, gamma=0):
  B10 tool: 1069.80   reference: 1069.80   delta: 0.00  PASS
  B50 tool: 1499.56   reference: 1499.56   delta: 0.00  PASS
  B90 tool: 1859.64   reference: 1859.64   delta: 0.00  PASS
  MTTF tool: 1479.60  reference: 1479.60   delta: 0.00  PASS


4.2  MLE OPTIMIZER (L-BFGS-B)
-------------------------------
Functions: LBFGSB.minimize, numericalGradient, projectGradient, wolfeLineSearch

SOURCE REVIEW:
- Numerical gradient uses central differences with h = max(1e-7, |x_i|*1e-5).
  O(h²) accurate — correct choice for smooth log-likelihood.
- wolfeLineSearch: c1=1e-4 (Armijo), c2=0.9 (strong Wolfe). Both within
  standard ranges (Nocedal & Wright 2006).
- L-BFGS two-loop recursion with m=6 memory pairs — standard implementation.
- Hessian scaling H0 = s'y/y'y — Shanno (1978) scaling — correct.
- Multi-start: 4 starting points (Dubey initialiser, x0.5, x2, beta=1).
- maxIter=500 for full fit, 80 for fast scan — reasonable.

ASSESSMENT: CORRECT.

STATUS: No change in v11.1. Verified correct in v11.0 audit.

VALIDATION (Dataset 1):
  tool beta: 5.5785   scipy: 5.5785   delta: <1e-4  PASS
  tool eta:  1601.40  scipy: 1601.41  delta: 0.01   PASS


4.3  NEGATIVE LOG-LIKELIHOOD
-----------------------------
Function: negLogLikGeneral

  exact:    log(beta/eta) + (beta-1)*log((t-gamma)/eta) - ((t-gamma)/eta)^beta
  right:    -((t-gamma)/eta)^beta
  interval: log(R(left) - R(right))

ASSESSMENT: CORRECT. Standard MLE for Weibull with mixed censoring (Lawless
2003, Section 3.2). Guard for prob <= 0 uses Number.EPSILON — appropriate.

STATUS: No change in v11.1.


4.4  3-PARAMETER MLE (Grid + Golden-Section)
---------------------------------------------
Functions: weibullMLE3, refineGammaLocal

  Step 1: Coarse grid of 201 points over gamma in [0, minT * 0.9999].
  Step 2: Golden-section over [bestGamma +/- minT/200] with 25 iterations.
  Step 3: Full multi-start MLE at refined gamma.
  Step 4: Fallback to 2P if beta < 1 AND gamma > 0.9 * minT.

ASSESSMENT: CORRECT overall.

STATUS: No change in v11.1. DEFECT-03 (golden-section bracket width) remains
open — see Section 9.

4.7  KOLMOGOROV-SMIRNOV STATISTIC AND P-VALUE
-----------------------------------------------
  lambda = (sqrt(n) + 0.12 + 0.11/sqrt(n)) * D   [Stephens 1977 correction]
  p = 2 * sum_{k=1}^{60} (-1)^(k+1) * exp(-2k^2*lambda^2)

ASSESSMENT: CORRECT.

STATUS: No change in v11.1.

NOTE: KS with estimated parameters is anti-conservative (Lilliefors bias),
acknowledged in the tool UI — not a bug.


4.8  ANDERSON-DARLING STATISTIC
--------------------------------
*** DEFECT-01 — RESOLVED IN v11.1 ***

ORIGINAL DEFECT (v11.0):
  ADstatistic() — used exclusively by Monte Carlo GoF — paired log(F[i]) with
  log(1-F[n-1-i]), reading two different positions. This produced AD values
  approximately 52x larger than the correct value.
  Example: Dataset 1, correct A² = 0.2053; buggy result = 10.6620.

REMEDIATION APPLIED (v11.1):
  ADstatistic() now uses the canonical formula — same as weibullMLE():

    for (let i = 0; i < n; i++) {
      const Fc = clamp(weibullCDF(sortedData[i], eta, beta, gamma));
      A += (2*i+1) * Math.log(Fc) + (2*(n-i)-1) * Math.log(1-Fc);
    }
    return -n - A/n;

VERIFICATION:
  Dataset 1 (n=10, beta=5.578, eta=1601.4):
    v11.0 ADstatistic():   10.6620  (WRONG)
    v11.1 ADstatistic():    0.2053  (CORRECT)
    Canonical reference:    0.2053  PASS

IMPACT OF FIX:
  - The "obs A²" shown in the Monte Carlo GoF result panel is now correct.
  - MC GoF p-values are now statistically valid: both obsAD and simAD are
    computed with the same correct formula, and the comparison count/N is
    a valid parametric bootstrap test (Stephens 1986).

STATUS: RESOLVED. (v11.1)


4.9  ANDERSON-DARLING P-VALUE
-------------------------------
*** DEFECT-02 — RESOLVED IN v11.1 ***

ORIGINAL DEFECT (v11.0):
  adPvalueMarsaglia() implements the Marsaglia & Marsaglia (2004) series for
  the A² distribution under NORMALITY with KNOWN parameters — a categorically
  different null distribution from Weibull with estimated parameters.
  Result: well-fitting Weibull data showed p ~ 1.3e-6 instead of p > 0.25.

REMEDIATION APPLIED (v11.1):
  GoF display block refactored. The AD significance is now assessed via
  Stephens (1974) Table 5 critical values specific to the Weibull distribution:

    A²* < 0.474  ->  "p > 0.25 (good fit)"      [green]
    A²* < 0.637  ->  "0.10 < p < 0.25"          [green]
    A²* < 0.757  ->  "0.05 < p < 0.10"          [yellow]
    A²* < 1.038  ->  "0.01 < p < 0.05"          [yellow]
    A²* >= 1.038 ->  "p < 0.01 (poor fit)"      [red]

  The displayed statistic is now labeled "AD A²*" (Stephens-corrected).
  adPvalueMarsaglia() is retained internally for Excel export only.

VERIFICATION:
  Dataset 1, A²* = 0.2182:
    v11.0: displayed "p = 1.3e-06"  (WRONG — catastrophically misleading)
    v11.1: displays "p > 0.25 (good fit)"  (CORRECT per Stephens 1974)
    Traffic light: RED in v11.0 -> GREEN in v11.1  PASS

STATUS: RESOLVED. (v11.1)

4.14  LIKELIHOOD-RATIO TEST 2P VS 3P (SELF & LIANG 1987)
----------------------------------------------------------
  D = 2*(ll3 - ll2)
  p = 1 - normalCDF(sqrt(D))

Algebraically equivalent to 0.5*(1-chi2.cdf(D,1)) — boundary-corrected
per Self & Liang (1987). Verified numerically: D=1.229 -> p=0.1338. CORRECT.

STATUS: No change in v11.1.

COMPLETED (implemented in v11.1):

  R1. [DONE] Fix ADstatistic() canonical formula (DEFECT-01).
  R2. [DONE] Replace misleading AD p-value with Stephens (1974) bands (DEFECT-02).
  R3. [DONE] Empty form on page load (DEFECT-09).
  R4. [DONE] Add "New" button for clean-slate reset.

REMAINING (recommended for a future release):

  R5. Widen 3P golden-section bracket (DEFECT-03):
      After the coarse grid, perform a secondary full golden-section pass over
      [0, minT*0.9999]. Minimal computational cost; eliminates the risk of
      missing true gamma in flat likelihood regions.

  R6. Resolve UI language inconsistency (DEFECT-07):
      Translate "Bootstrap 3P completo" panel labels and warnings to English.

  R7. Add overflow guard on input times (DEFECT-08):
      Warn if any failure time exceeds 1e8, or if the exponent (t/eta)^beta
      would exceed ~709 (double precision overflow boundary).

  R8. Document Excel import behavior (DEFECT-06):
      Add "(last row only)" to the Load Excel button tooltip or help text.

INFORMATIONAL:

  I1. The bias correction formula (n-0.68)/n is an approximation. For critical
      small-sample applications (n <= 10, beta > 3), consult the exact tables
      in Thoman, Bain & Antle (1969).

  I2. Eta bias for small n (DEFECT-04) is not corrected. Common practice in
      reliability tools, but should be disclosed in the documentation.

  I3. The 3P fallback condition (beta < 1 AND gamma > 0.9*minT) may not catch
      all instability cases. Monitor convergence warnings in production use.

  I4. adPvalueMarsaglia() is still present in the codebase and writes its
      value to the Excel export column "AD p (analytical)". Consider adding
      a cell comment warning that this value uses the Marsaglia (2004) normal-
      distribution formula and is NOT valid for Weibull distribution inference.

12. CONCLUSION
================================================================================

The Weibull Analysis Tool v11.1 is a sophisticated, professionally implemented
reliability analysis application whose two critical statistical defects have been
fully corrected in this release.

The Anderson-Darling formula in the Monte Carlo GoF module now produces correct
values (0.2053 vs the erroneous 10.6620 for Dataset 1), restoring the validity
of simulation-based goodness-of-fit p-values. The analytical AD significance
display now uses Weibull-specific Stephens (1974) critical values, showing
"p > 0.25 (good fit)" with a green traffic light for well-fitting data instead
of the catastrophically misleading "p ~ 1.3e-06" with a red indicator that
appeared in v11.0.

Two UX improvements (blank form on load; "New" button) meaningfully reduce
friction for day-to-day engineering use.

All other core statistical components validated as correct in the v11.0 audit
remain correct and unchanged: the L-BFGS-B MLE optimizer, KS test, displayed
AD statistic in weibullMLE(), profile likelihood and Fisher CIs, the Self &
Liang LR test, Herd-Johnson ranks, linear regression, parametric bootstrap,
mixture detection, and the Gamma/Normal auxiliary functions.

The four remaining open defects (DEFECT-03 through -08, excluding resolved ones)
are MODERATE or MINOR in severity and do not affect correctness for typical
reliability engineering datasets. The tool is ready for engineering analysis,
academic use, and training purposes at this quality level.

For critical safety or regulatory decisions, cross-validation with ReliaSoft
Weibull++, JMP, or Minitab remains advisable, consistent with the tool's own
footer disclaimer.
