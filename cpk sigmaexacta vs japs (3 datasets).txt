1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This report documents the validation testing of the SigmaExacta Process Capability Analysis tool against JASP (Jeffreys's Amazing Statistics Program) using a new dataset with three distinct subgroups. The analysis focused on comparing process performance metrics (Pp, Ppk), overall statistical parameters, and expected failure rates between both statistical platforms.

2. OBJECTIVE
The objective of this validation was to:

Verify the accuracy of process performance indices (Pp, Ppk) for a multi-dataset scenario

Compare overall standard deviation estimates and central tendency measures

Assess consistency in confidence interval calculations for performance metrics

Validate the calculation of expected failure rates and non-conformance statistics

Evaluate the handling of non-normal process data

6. ANALYSIS
Process Performance Indices (Pp, Ppk): Excellent agreement between both tools with virtually identical point estimates. The differences (<0.1%) are well within acceptable limits for statistical software comparisons.

Confidence Intervals: SigmaExacta's 95% confidence intervals show excellent alignment with JASP's 90% confidence intervals for both Pp and Ppk. The ranges overlap substantially, indicating consistent estimation of variability in performance metrics.

Expected Failures: Both tools report identical expected failure rates (118,641 ppm), representing approximately 11.86% defective parts. This perfect alignment demonstrates consistency in the calculation of non-conformance statistics.

Normality Assessment: Both tools indicate non-normal data distribution. SigmaExacta reports failing Shapiro-Wilk and Anderson-Darling tests, while JASP reports Anderson-Darling p-value of 0, confirming non-normality.

Within-Group Variation: JASP reports an overall within-group standard deviation of 0.538, while SigmaExacta focuses on individual dataset analyses without reporting an overall within-group metric. This represents different analytical approaches rather than a discrepancy.

Process Performance vs Capability: Both tools correctly differentiate between performance (Pp/Ppk based on total variation) and capability (Cp/Cpk based on within variation), with SigmaExacta providing detailed dataset-specific capability metrics.
